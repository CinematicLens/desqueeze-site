What is anamorphic de-squeeze and why fix distortion?
Anamorphic lenses squeeze the image horizontally to capture a wider field of view on a normal sensor. This unique look is popular with professional filmmakers and those utilizing affordable mobile anamorphic lenses (like those from Moment, Sirui, or Moondog Labs) on their iPhone or Android devices. Without de-squeezing, footage looks tall and skinny. This online anamorphic de-squeeze tool fixes the anamorphic distortion, stretching the footage back to its intended cinematic aspect ratio (e.g., 2.39:1) so your shots look professional instead of stretched.

What is anamorphic de-squeeze and why fix distortion?

Anamorphic lenses squeeze the image horizontally to capture a wider field of view on a normal sensor. This unique look is popular with professional filmmakers and those using affordable mobile anamorphic lenses (Moment, Sirui, Moondog Labs) on iPhone or Android. Without de-squeezing, footage looks tall and skinny. This online anamorphic de-squeeze tool fixes the distortion and stretches footage back to the intended cinematic aspect ratio (e.g. 2.39:1).

Desqueeze settings for 1.33× lenses

For 1.33× anamorphic (many Moment and classic adapters): in the Web Studio choose the 1.33× (Classic) preset. Preview updates instantly; export uses the same factor. Batch up to 10 files. Same preset is available in our iOS app and Android / Windows apps.

Desqueeze settings for 1.5×, 1.8× and 2×

For 1.5×, 1.8× or 2× anamorphic lenses (e.g. Sirui, some cinema glass): select the matching preset in the dropdown. Use Custom if your lens has a non-standard squeeze. Export MP4, JPG or PNG with the correct aspect ratio for editing or delivery.

Desqueezing in Premiere, DaVinci or Final Cut vs our tool

You can de-squeeze manually in an NLE (e.g. scale horizontally or change pixel aspect ratio). For quick previews, client links, or batch exports without opening a project, the Web Studio and our apps are faster: upload, pick ratio, export. Use the NLE for full grading and finishing; use our tool as a pre-processor when you need corrected files quickly.
